Ford GoBike System Data Analysis and Exploration¶

by Edem Doe Honu¶

Introduction¶

The Ford GoBike System dataset provides detailed information/data on the greater San Francisco Bay in regard to individual bike usage/ride in a bike sharing system.

This document therefore explores the Ford GoBike System dataset to create meaningful visualizations (using both single and multi-variables), formulate insights, and draw cogent conclusions.

What is the structure of your dataset?¶

The dataset is made up of a total of 183412 observations originally characterized under 16 dinstintive columns and after cleaning reduced to 11 columns: duration_sec, bike_id, member_birth_year, user_type, member_birth_year, member_gender,end_station_name, bike_share_for_all_trip, start_time, end_time and age_bikers. Majority of the column variables are numeric in nature.

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

I am most interested in finding the correlation between member_gender, age_bikers, user_type, bike shares members and duration of trips.

I am also interested in how the several variables such as member_gender, age_bikers, user_type, bike shares members correlate specifically/individually with the trip duration.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

  1. Member gender
  2. Ages of the bikers
  3. User types (Customer/Subscriber)
  4. Duration of the trips
  5. Bike Share Program member

  1. Because of the age outliers and the invalidity of the data in the age coloumns, I filtered out ages greater than 50 years for my analysis/visualization.

  2. The distribution of the trip duration in minutes was also quite interesting since shorter trip duration recorded higher frequency. This could signal a higher bike usage for short-duration trips but not long-duration trips.

  3. The age interval of the bike users was also quite interesting creating an insight that middle-aged users mostly purchace the bike services.
